# Assumptions and Limitations

This project models hospital food production and overproduction waste using standardized operational assumptions. The goal is to estimate the **order of magnitude and distribution** of food waste under typical kitchen practices, not to calculate exact loss.

---

## Key Assumptions

### Standardized Tray Model
Each patient meal tray is modeled as containing:
- 4 oz protein  
- 4 oz starch  
- 4 oz vegetable  

**Total modeled weight:** 12 oz per tray

---

### Uniform Production Buffer
A **10% production buffer** is assumed across all meals to account for:
- demand uncertainty  
- late trays  
- census changes  
- operational variability  

---

### Waste Scenarios
Three waste scenarios are modeled to reflect a range of operational outcomes:
- **Low:** 5%  
- **Expected:** 10%  
- **High:** 18%  

---

### Portion Consistency
Portion sizes are treated as consistent across units and meal periods for modeling purposes.

---

### Scope of Waste Modeled
Estimated waste represents **overproduction waste only** (food prepared but not required to meet ordered demand).

---

## Limitations

This analysis does **not** measure:
- Plate waste from partially eaten meals  
- Food returned due to patient illness or discharge timing  
- Spoilage from inventory expiration  
- Prep errors, rework, or repurposing  
- Labor efficiency or workflow allocation  

Additional limitations include:
- No direct food weights were recorded; all weight estimates are derived from standardized portion assumptions  
- Results should be interpreted as **directional indicators**, not exact waste totals  

---

## Data Gaps and Future Measurement Needs

To quantify pre-production and post-production waste more precisely, additional data would be required, including:
- Inventory purchase and spoilage logs  
- Tray return tracking by unit and meal  
- Portion variance measurement  
- Patient preference and substitution records
